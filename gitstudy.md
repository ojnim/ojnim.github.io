[How to write a good pull request & comment]

https://github.blog/2015-01-21-how-to-write-the-perfect-pull-request/

1. Include the purpose of this pull request
2. Consider providing an overview of why the work is taking place
3. Remember that anyone in the company could be reading this Pull Request, so the content and tone may inform people other than those taking part, now or later
4. Be explicit about what feedback you want
5. Be explicit about when you want feedback
6. @mention individuals that you specifically want to involve in the discussion, and mention why
7. @mention teams that you want to involve in the discussion, and mention why


https://medium.com/@yukas/how-to-write-good-pull-requests-comments-ce17ec3f5f36

1. Descriptive
2. Has the "why" part
3. Polite


https://dev.to/tan/effective-comments-on-pull-requests-g46

1. Leave more questions than suggestions
2. Leaving compliments is also good
3. If it's too big of a change, just mention it and discuss it further.
4. Don't hide answers
5. Avoid using "YOU" as mush as possible
